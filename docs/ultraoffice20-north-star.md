# UltraOffice2.0 North Star

This is the plain-English doctrine for UltraOffice2.0. Do not drift from this without an explicit product decision.

UltraOffice2.0 is an AI-assisted operating system for Carnes and Sons Air Conditioning.

It helps run the company from the first customer call or text all the way through scheduling, dispatching, technician work, estimates, approvals, installs, invoicing, warranty, rebates, inspections, and follow-up.

The guiding idea:

**Humans talk, sell, help, and approve. JARVIS tracks the workflow and keeps the company from dropping balls.**

## Operating Headquarters

### Intake HQ

Intake HQ is where calls and texts come in.

Its job is to quickly answer:

- Who is this?
- What do they need?
- Are they an existing customer?
- Do they have active work?
- Do we need to call, text, book, or follow up?

Intake must stay communication-focused. It should not become a giant task board.

### Now HQ

Now HQ is the "what needs attention right now" system.

JARVIS watches workflows and creates or updates action cards when something needs a human. It should not show every step of every process. It should show the things that are stuck, ready, overdue, uncertain, or need approval.

### Dispatch HQ

Dispatch HQ runs the day.

It owns jobs, technicians, route and ETA context, active work, and real-time field updates. It should show what is happening on the board and what technicians are reporting from the field.

### Tech Mobile View

The technician app is for the person at the job.

The technician needs to:

- See where they are going.
- Contact the customer.
- Mark on the way, on site, paused, and finished.
- Take photos.
- Talk to JARVIS.
- Document findings.
- Add repairs or replacement options.
- Send something clear to the customer for approval.

### Quote And Presentation System

Estimates must become customer-facing sales presentations.

The cart alone does not sell the job. The presentation should explain comfort, reliability, efficiency, warranty, features, benefits, urgency, financing, and approval options.

### Customer HQ

Customer HQ is the master customer record.

It owns jobs, estimates, attachments, calls, texts, Comfort Club membership, warranty, installation history, and follow-up.

### Team HQ

Team HQ is for internal team communication and shared resources.

Employee and vendor communication should not clutter customer intake.

## Workflow Backbone

The app must support these workflows:

- Estimate workflow.
- Install workflow.
- Service workflow.
- Technician field workflow.
- Lead and quote drip follow-up workflow.
- Closeout work: warranty, CPS rebate, city inspection, quality check.

JARVIS should understand these workflows, update cards as new information comes in, and avoid creating duplicate or stale work.

If a customer calls back and changes the situation, JARVIS should update the existing card instead of creating a new mess.

No action is allowed to disappear just because someone clicked accept. Every quote, job, estimate, follow-up, paperwork question, CPS rebate question, warranty question, permit item, inspection item, or customer promise must land somewhere with an owner.

Ownership can be either:

- A named person, when the work needs a calendar block, visit, quote, callback, estimate, job, or personal follow-up.
- A shared office queue, when the work is paperwork, closeout, CPS rebate, warranty, permit, inspection, billing cleanup, or another oddball office item.

JARVIS should choose the likely owner, but the human must be able to override it. Person-owned work must be scheduled before it leaves Now HQ. Office-queue work should stay visible until someone marks it handled.

## System Summary

UltraOffice2.0 is a communication-first HVAC command center where every phone call, text, job, quote, technician update, and customer decision flows into one tracked customer journey.

The AI watches the whole process, keeps the next step visible, and asks humans to approve important actions instead of making the team manually track every little thing.
